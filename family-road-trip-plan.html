Suggestions

The itinerary is strong, but it can be sharper. The main opportunity is to trade low-value famous stops for places that are easier with teens, cheaper, more weather-resilient, and more distinctive to each city.

Best add: Lancaster Central Market Best NYC backup: Transit Museum Best free skyline: Staten Island Ferry or Roosevelt Island Tram Best skip: NYC Amish Market

High-Value Adds Add if time allows

  • Lancaster Central Market: better local food/culture than NYC Amish Market. Add Friday morning June 12 before Hershey if park opening is later, or Saturday morning before Amish Country.
  • New York Transit Museum: excellent rainy-day or heat-day swap for kids/teens. Current admission is modest, with adults at $10 and children 2-17 at $5, and advance tickets are encouraged.
  • Grand Central plus New York Public Library/Bryant Park: classic, mostly free, and easy to slot into Midtown without adding much cost.
  • Brooklyn Bridge Park/DUMBO: better skyline payoff than many paid observatories. Add only if June 10 is going smoothly after Lower Manhattan.
  • Roosevelt Island Tram: a cheap, memorable skyline ride that works better than an expensive observatory if you want a quick "NYC view" moment.
  • National Archives in DC: if you skip a Smithsonian, the Declaration/Constitution fit the history arc of Gettysburg and Independence Hall.

Food You Might Be Missing Family-friendly

  • Upper West Side breakfast: add Zabar's, Levain Bakery, or Absolute Bagels style stops near the hotel so breakfast does not create subway backtracking.
  • NYC tacos: Los Tacos No. 1 in Chelsea Market is usually a better family value than a full sit-down lunch there.
  • Pizza strategy: Made in New York Pizza is done. John's of Bleecker can stay as a small shared try if the family still cares, but do not spend another prime dinner slot chasing pizza.
  • Katz's strategy: go mid-afternoon and split sandwiches. Treat it as an experience/snack, not a full expensive meal for five.
  • Lancaster ice cream: add Fox Meadows Creamery if route timing works. It fits the family food preferences better than another heavy restaurant meal.
  • Amish Country lunch: Dienner's, Katie's Kitchen, Bird-in-Hand Bakery/Cafe, or Shady Maple. Shady Maple is touristy but efficient if the family wants the big buffet experience.

Overrated or Risky Trim first

  • Times Square: worth one short night walk, not a long block. It is crowded, expensive, and weaker than almost every neighborhood walk on the list.
  • Serendipity near Times Square: fun idea, but the value is mostly dessert nostalgia. Keep only if you reserve and treat it as dessert, not a destination dinner.
  • Sephora Times Square: if shopping time is limited, this is the easiest store to skip. It is not meaningfully different from other locations, just busier.
  • FAO Schwarz: good for 20-30 minutes if nearby, but do not route the day around it.
  • Empire State/paid observatory: expensive for five. Use Roosevelt Island Tram, Brooklyn Bridge Park, or Staten Island Ferry unless a paid skyline deck is a family must.
  • Au Cheval: strong burger reputation, but wait/cost can be poor for a family road trip. Cabalar in Lancaster better matches the trip's burger goal.
